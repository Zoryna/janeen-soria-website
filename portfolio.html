Girls Who Code

I was a volunteer for Girls Who Code's Loyola chapter during the spring and fall semester of 2018. I helped girls in grades 6th to 12th learn HTML and CSS, and I mentored a group of three students with the website they created for their semester project. I spoke in a Loyola seminar, COMP 399, to speak about the organization and its chapter at Loyola University Chicago.

DON'T PANIC

DON'T PANIC CS is a computer science club at Loyola University Chicago that was just founded in the Fall 2018 semester. The club was founded to create a computer science community witin our university to help computer science students, or students interested in tech or computer science, and their growth toward their careers.

As one of the board members, I assist in organizing meetings and information and recruiting new members. I help members with time management, project management, and networking.

Here in the photo are the board members. From left to right it is Lucas Perez (President and Founder), Makenna Walsh (Treasurer), Linette Maliakal (Secretary), Janeen Soria (Vice President), and Spencer Johnston (Recruiter).
